📋 What happened

He stopped eating one day. Then everything went wrong.

Billu is the kind of cat who shows up at the same spot every morning, waiting for his person. For five years, that person has been Ranjana, his caretaker in Mumbai, who feeds him and the other community cats in her area, every single day without fail.

In early April, Billu stopped showing up for meals. When he did come around, he wouldn't eat. Then the vomiting started. Ranjana took him to a vet nearby, but things kept getting worse. He had seizures, his temperature dropped, and he was fading fast.

She rushed him to Top Dog Pets Clinic in Andheri, where the team ran X-rays, ultrasounds, and blood work. The results were bad.

🔬 His ultrasound showed a severely distended stomach and intestines filled with fluid, with reverse motility, meaning his gut had essentially stopped moving food forward. His white blood cell count was at 26,200 (nearly double the upper limit), with signs of serious internal inflammation. The vets flagged GI obstruction and recommended immediate surgery.

When imaging alone couldn't pinpoint the exact blockage, the surgical team performed an Exploratory Laparotomy. They had to open him up to find out what was going on inside. The procedure performed was called an Enterotomy, where the surgeon cuts into the intestine to remove whatever's causing the blockage.

What they found was deeply impacted material lodged at multiple points in his intestines. His intestinal wall was already fragile from how long the obstruction had been there. The vet noted paralytic ileus, meaning his bowel had shut down completely.

His prognosis was listed as "guarded." That's the medical way of saying: we don't know if he'll make it.

🏥 The recovery

He spent days in an incubator. But he's a fighter.

Billu made it through the surgery. He made it through the ICU. He's home now, resting in a cage, slowly starting to eat again. The hard part is behind him.

But the bill isn't.

₹1,50,000. That's what it took to save his life. Diagnostics, surgery, ICU, medication, follow-up care. Ranjana is a community caretaker, not a wealthy pet parent. She didn't think twice about getting him the care he needed, but she can't shoulder this alone.
